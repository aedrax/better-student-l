  • On-Campus Tobacco Cessation Program

    Good afternoon! This is a reminder regarding the *free* *tobacco cessation program* that ONU HealthWise is providing to the campus. It has been created to help with *all forms* of tobacco, not just cigarette smoking! Participants will be able to receive a wide variety of *services* through this program including: - Individual, personalized counseling from the ONU HealthWise team - Continual follow-up visits, encouragement, and support - Group Support sessions - Nicotine replacement therapy provided free in the form of a patch, gum, or lozenge as chosen by the participant and pharmacist So who is *eligible* to take advantage of this wonderful opportunity? Participants must be *>* 18 years old and either an: - ONU employee (including Sodexo and Barnes & Noble), - Immediate family member of the employee, or - A student An inclusive list of products *banned* on campus can be found at www.onu.edu/tobaccofree, but includes: - *cigarettes*, *electronic cigarettes*, cigars - pipe tobacco, hookah tobacco, *chewing tobacco*, *dipping tobacco*, snuff With successful completion of the tobacco cessation program, *5 Healthy Campus Points* will be earned. If you are interested in the program or have questions regarding this opportunity, please call or email ONU HealthWise. - Email: healthwise@onu.edu - Phone: 419-772-2299 (Brittany Long, PharmD) *We are here to help you!* -- *Brittany L.     Hello everyone, Circle K, a service organization here on campus, is selling shirts and the proceeds will go to UNICEF to prevent maternal and neonatal tetanus. Since Circle K and Kiwanis has partnered with UNICEF, we have immunized more than 130 million women in over 53 countries and have completely eliminated the disease in 37 countries.   • Remembering September 11th

    Remembering September 11th: On Friday, our nation will remember the 14th anniversary of the attacks of September 11, 2001. Many of us on campus can remember where we were when we first heard of the sad events in New York, Pennsylvania, and Washington, D.C. Many of our students on campus were young schoolchildren or even in preschool at the time. All of us have been affected by the aftermath of that heartrending day, as we have witnessed heightened security at home and abroad, including deployments of our military personnel in Iraq and Afghanistan. This year, as has become our custom, we will be ringing the Chapel bells at the exact times when the four flights of 9/11 came to their tragic end. You will hear a single tone from the Chapel carillon at the following times: 8:46 a.m. Flight 11 hit the North Tower of the World Trade Center 9:03 a.m. Flight 175 hit the South Tower of the World Trade Center 9:37 a.m. Flight 77 crashed into the western fa=C3=A7ade of the Pentagon 10:07 a.m. Flight 93 crashed in Somerset County, Pennsylvania I would encourage you to pause for a moment of reflection and prayer as you hear these bells on Friday morning, and remember the sacrifice of those who lost their lives on September 11th: on the planes, in the World Trade Center and Pentagon, and among the New York City fire and police departments. As always, the Chapel sanctuary, prayer room, and multi-faith room will be open throughout the day for those who wish to pray. Blessings, Chaplain David MacDonald "Preach the gospel at all times.
